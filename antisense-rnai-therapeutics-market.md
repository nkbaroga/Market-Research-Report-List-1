<p><p>The Antisense & RNAi Therapeutics market encompasses various types of RNA-based therapies. RNA interference (RNAi) utilizes small interfering RNA (siRNA) to selectively degrade target mRNA, reducing protein synthesis. MicroRNA (miRNA) therapies involve regulating gene expression by mimicking or inhibiting endogenous miRNAs. Antisense RNA therapies use single-stranded RNA molecules to bind to complementary mRNA, preventing translation. Each modality offers unique mechanisms to modulate gene expression, presenting opportunities for targeted treatments in genetic disorders and cancers.</p></p>
