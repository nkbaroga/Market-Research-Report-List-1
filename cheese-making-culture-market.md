<p><p>Cheese-making cultures are essential for fermentation and flavor development in cheese production. The mesophilic type thrives at moderate temperatures, producing milder cheeses like cheddar and gouda. In contrast, thermophilic cultures operate at higher temperatures, resulting in sharp cheeses such as mozzarella and parmesan. Probiotic cultures focus on health benefits, enriching cheese with beneficial bacteria that promote gut health. These different culture types address varied consumer preferences, leading to a diverse cheese market catering to taste, texture, and health-conscious choices.</p></p>
