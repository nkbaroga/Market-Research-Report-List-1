<p><p>A drug-coated stent is a small tube made of metal or polymer that is implanted into narrowed or blocked coronary arteries to improve blood flow. These stents are coated with medication that is slowly released to prevent the re-narrowing of the artery, a condition known as restenosis. This innovative solution has significantly improved treatment outcomes in patients with coronary artery disease, leading to their widespread adoption in interventional cardiology.</p><p>The global drug-coated stent market is poised for robust growth, driven by factors such as the rising prevalence of cardiovascular diseases, advancements in stent technology, and increasing demand for minimally invasive procedures. The growing geriatric population, which is more susceptible to heart ailments, further fuels market expansion. A notable trend is the development of bioresorbable drug-coated stents, which dissolve over time, offering potential advantages over traditional stents. Additionally, increasing investments in healthcare infrastructure and rising awareness about cardiac health contribute to market dynamics.</p><p>The Drug Coated Stent Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 9.4% during the forecast period, reflecting strong demand and innovation in this critical area of cardiovascular treatment.</p></p>

<p><p>The drug-coated stent market features several key types, each designed to prevent restenosis in coronary arteries. Everolimus-coated stents release the drug everolimus, promoting localized healing. Zotarolimus-coated stents utilize zotarolimus for similar therapeutic effects. Ridaforolimus-coated stents leverage ridaforolimus, targeting inflammation and cell proliferation. The SYNERGY coated stent combines biodegradable polymers with everolimus, facilitating healing over time. Orsiro coated stents use a unique biodegradable polymer to enhance drug delivery and minimize late adverse events, contributing to overall cardiovascular health.</p></p>
