<p><p>The hospital corridor door market consists of two primary types: manual doors and automatic doors. Manual doors require physical effort to open and close, often suitable for smaller areas or budget-conscious facilities. They offer simplicity and reliability but may pose accessibility challenges. In contrast, automatic doors enhance convenience and hygiene, as they open with sensors, reducing contact and facilitating smooth patient flow, especially in high-traffic areas. The choice between these types depends on hospital size, budget, and specific operational needs.</p></p>
