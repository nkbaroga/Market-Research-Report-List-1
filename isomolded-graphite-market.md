<p><ul><li>CIP Method</li><li>Vibration Molding Method</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The isomolded graphite market features two primary manufacturing methods: the Cold Isostatic Pressing (CIP) method and vibration molding. The CIP method involves applying uniform pressure via a liquid medium, resulting in dense graphite parts with excellent mechanical properties and isotropic characteristics. Conversely, the vibration molding method utilizes vibrations to consolidate graphite powders in a mold, enhancing material flow and density. Both methods cater to different applications in industries such as aerospace, automotive, and electronics, driving innovation and efficiency in production.</p></p>
