<p><p>The laboratory monocular microscopes market features two main types: inverted and upright microscopes. Inverted microscopes are designed with the objective lenses positioned above the stage, allowing for observation of samples from below, making them ideal for cell culture and live specimen studies. Upright microscopes, on the other hand, have lenses below the stage, suitable for examining flat samples like slides. Both types serve specific research needs, impacting their demand and application in various laboratory settings.</p></p>
